Lemon Squares

Back to Homepage

Lemon squares, also called lemon bars, is a delicious dessert. Below you will find a list of ingredients and a step-by-step guide.

The following recipe is directly from Preppy Kitchen, because in no way am I a baker. I simply code websites, and this is a TOP project. Here is a link to the recipe.

Ingredients

  • butter
  • eggs
  • salt
  • sugar
  • powdered sugar
  • lemons

You may be asking yourself, "What are the measurements and portions for this recipe?". Great question! I have no effing idea. This recipe does not have that info. Good luck.

Baking Equipment

  • 9x13 inch baking dish
  • parchment paper

How to Make Lemon Squares

  1. Set your oven to 350F. While the oven is preheating, line your baking dish with parchment paper. Add flour, salt, and powdered sugar to a bowl and whisk together.
  2. Melt your butter and pour it in. Perhaps add a teaspoon of vanilla. Do what your heart desires.
  3. Mix the batter with a spoon or spatula. Original recipe suggests crumbling the dough in your hands after it is mixed if you are doing this by hand.
  4. Evenly press the dough down with your fingers on your baking dish. Do not worry about the thickness.
  5. Bake for about 20 minutes or until the dough is light golden. This is kind of up to personal preference.
  6. Zest three lemons and mix it with sugar until fully incorporated. Sugar should be fragrant and light yellow when finished.
  7. Juice the lemons until you have a cup of lemon juice.
  8. Add the lemon sugar and half a cup of flour to a large bowl. Whisk together.
  9. Whisk in the eggs and lemon juice until fully incorporated.
  10. Pour the filling onto your dough base. Bake for about 25 minutes, rotating the pan halfway through.
  11. Let the squares cool for about an hour, then chill in the refridgerator for about two hours.
  12. Once chilled, remove from pan and peel away parchment paper. Dust the powdered sugar on top.
  13. Use a sharp and damp knife to cut the squares. For best results, re-dampen the knife after each cut. If you're gonna be extra like that.
